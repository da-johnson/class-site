The Future of Low-Earth Orbit

Retirement of the International Space Station

Space stations play a vital role in researching efforts for manufacturing in space and general research. With the planned retirement of the International Space Station (ISS) in 2031, its contributions will be sorely missed. However, commercial interest in space is increasing, with new space stations being developed by various companies. NASA has signed agreements with Axiom, Blue Origin, Nanoracks LLC, and Northrop Grumman Systems Corporation to develop designs for space stations and other commercial destinations in space. The total estimated award amount for all four funded Space Act Agreements is $415.6 million. These agreements will enable a robust, American-led commercial economy in low-Earth orbit, maintaining an uninterrupted U.S. presence in space.

More information on each commercial space station can be found in the sections below, along with an image that links to each project's home site.

Stations on the Horizon

Axiom

As the construction of the Axiom Space Station progresses, with a targeted launch date in late 2025, this next-generation platform in low-Earth orbit promises to open new frontiers in various research fields. One area of particular interest is pharmaceutical research, where microgravity offers unique opportunities to study biological processes and develop novel drugs.

In microgravity, muscle and bone tissues experience accelerated atrophy, making space an ideal environment for testing drugs that prevent or reverse tissue loss. Research performed in microgravity has already contributed to the FDA approval and marketing of several drugs, highlighting the potential of this environment to drive biomedical advancements.

Myostatin, a muscle secreted factor that negatively regulates muscle cell growth, is one focus of pharmaceutical research on the space station. Mighty mice, genetically engineered to have disrupted myostatin function, maintain their preflight muscle mass in space. This discovery has led to the development of antibody-based drugs targeting myostatin, which are currently in various stages of clinical trials.

Another promising drug under development is the Activin type IIB receptor decoy (ACVR2B/Fc). This novel antibody-based drug promotes growth and homeostasis of both muscle and bone. In microgravity, treatment with the ACVR2B/Fc drug has shown dramatic increases in muscle and bone mass, protecting these tissues against atrophy. Moreover, the drug has demonstrated the ability to reverse atrophied bone and muscle, offering hope for future treatments addressing aging, injury, disuse, or disease-induced atrophy.

Aside from pharmaceutical research, the Axiom Space Station will host a variety of other research areas, including protein crystallization, regenerative medicine, stem cells, organoids, spheroids, 3D bioprinting, and materials science. As the construction of this innovative platform continues, the potential for scientific breakthroughs in microgravity is becoming increasingly apparent.

Blue Origin: Orbital Reef

Orbital Reef is set to be the premier mixed-use space station in low Earth orbit (LEO) for commerce, research, and tourism by the end of this decade. This innovative platform is designed to provide end-to-end services, standard interfaces, and technical support to new customers, opening doors to new markets and catalyzing the growth of a vibrant space ecosystem. Orbital Reef's spacious modules, with Earth-facing windows and distinct living and working quarters, offer a comfortable and inspiring environment for travelers and researchers alike.

Orbital Reef's research facilities are equipped to accelerate future discoveries and the development of new industries. The platform offers a range of applications and markets, including media, entertainment, and manufacturing. It provides utilities and services to sustain businesses, such as power, cooling, high-bandwidth communications, and robotic servicing.

For research purposes, Orbital Reef offers everything from experiment accommodations to spacious laboratories with next-generation shared facilities. The platform aims to make it easier than ever for researchers to conduct physical, biological, or Earth science experiments, develop new products, or test exploration systems in orbit.

Space tourism is another key aspect of Orbital Reef, allowing visitors to experience the thrill of spaceflight and the breathtaking views of Earth from space. The platform offers tailored flight plans, training, and activities for both short visits and longer stays. Orbital Reef's development is supported by a team of experts, including Boeing, Redwire Space, Amazon Supply Chain, Amazon Web Services, Genesis Engineering Solutions, and Arizona State University.

Nanoracks: Starlab

Voyager Space and Airbus Defence and Space have joined forces to create Starlab, the first continuously crewed, free-flying commercial space station. Set to launch in 2028, Starlab aims to serve NASA, the European Space Agency (ESA), and a global clientele of researchers, ensuring a continued human presence in Low-Earth Orbit (LEO).

Designed to provide 100% of the International Space Station's payload capacity, Starlab will enable hundreds of experiments and technical investigations each year. The space station will house the George Washington Carver Science Park, the first of its kind in space, providing a collaborative environment for scientists and industry experts to share findings and utilize new technologies.

With a $160 million Space Act Agreement from NASA, Voyager and its operating company, Nanoracks, are working diligently to replace the International Space Station with Starlab. The innovative space station will launch as a fully functional science park, accelerating accessible science, research, and manufacturing opportunities for global customers.

In a groundbreaking partnership, Voyager has announced that Hilton will be the official hotel partner for Starlab. Hilton's expertise in hospitality will help design and develop crew suites aboard Starlab, enhancing the human experience in space for extended stays. This collaboration could also lead to advancements in sustainability and design efficiencies for future hotel owners in space and on Earth.

Starlab signifies a new era in space exploration, fostering scientific and commercial advancements while providing a unique and comfortable experience for astronauts. With the support of NASA and international partners, Starlab is poised to become the platform of choice for space researchers and explorers worldwide.

Northrop Grumman: Commercial Space Station

Northrop Grumman's vision for space-as-a-service aims to create an accessible, full-service commercial space station in Low-Earth Orbit (LEO), carrying forward the International Space Station (ISS) legacy while expanding into multiple markets for a new commercial economy. The company envisions a space marketplace that includes scientific activities, tourism, and more.

Their concept for a safe, reliable, and cost-effective commercial space station uses trusted, adaptable technology from their human spaceflight and space operations program. This approach, combined with Northrop Grumman's commercial spacecraft heritage, will result in a cost-efficient and dependable design that supports multiple markets with room for growth.

In December 2021, Northrop Grumman signed a Space Act Agreement with NASA, securing $125.6 million to design a LEO commercial space station. This project builds on decades of experience supporting NASA, defense, and commercial programs, leveraging flight-proven elements like the Cygnus spacecraft that provides cargo delivery to the ISS.

Northrop Grumman is collaborating with STAR HARBOR on market research and early development of astronaut training curricula for the space station concept. This partnership establishes STAR HARBOR's intention to use Northrop Grumman's space station as an LEO destination for on-orbit services and training. Customized astronaut training programs will ensure high standards of safety and customer service for commercial spaceflight training and mission implementation, supporting a thriving future for the new space economy.

By accelerating human and technology advancements within the commercial aerospace ecosystem, Northrop Grumman's modular, commercial LEO destination lays the foundation for a diverse space economy, spanning science, tourism, industrial experimentation, and infrastructure development beyond the initial design.
